// 01 — Research Program

How people learn —
and forget

01 ——

Skill Acquisition & Retention Theory

An integrated three-stage theory of learning, consolidation, and decay grounded in the ACT-R cognitive architecture. Applied to keystroke, menu, and procedural task performance with documented relearning asymmetries.

02 ——

Bayesian Hierarchical Learning Analytics

Hierarchical Bayesian models that pool information across individuals to generate precise real-time performance predictions for adaptive instruction pipelines. Deployed within the U.S. Army's GIFT platform.

03 ——

Intelligent Tutoring Systems

Design and evaluation of ITS for U.S. Navy and Air Force maintenance training. Developed domain models, learner models, and assessment frameworks in collaboration with Penn State's Applied Cognitive Science Lab.

04 ——

Physiological Cognitive Modeling

Integration of respiratory and physiological signals with ACT-R models of arousal regulation to predict performance in psychomotor tasks under stress. Applications to surgical and military operator training.

05 ——

Human Performance Under Stress

Modeling operator performance in time-constrained environments using cognitive architectures. Experimental and computational studies of attention, decision-making, and motor response under fatigue and time pressure.

06 ——

Advanced Manufacturing & ML

Parametric machine learning models for laser powder bed fusion processes. Bridging computational cognitive science and materials manufacturing at UCF's AMPAC research center.
